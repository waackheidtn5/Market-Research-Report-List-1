<p><p>The Lupin Seed market comprises various types, each with distinct characteristics and uses. **Lupinus Albus** (white lupin) is valued for its high protein content and is commonly used in food products. **Lupinus Luteus** (yellow lupin) has notable nutritional benefits and is often used in animal feed. **Lupinus Angustifolius** (narrow-leafed lupin) is recognized for its low alkaloid levels, making it suitable for direct human consumption. **Lupinus Caudatus** (tailing lupin) is primarily cultivated for its seeds. **Lupinus Mutabilis** (Andean lupin) is important in traditional diets in South America. Other varieties may include niche types cultivated for specific regional preferences or agricultural traits.</p></p>
