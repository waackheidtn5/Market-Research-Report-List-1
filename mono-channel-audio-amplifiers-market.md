<p><p>Mono channel audio amplifiers can be categorized into several types based on their design and functionality. </p><p>Class-A amplifiers provide high audio fidelity but are inefficient, generating heat. Class-B amplifiers improve efficiency but may introduce distortion at low volumes. Class-A/B amplifiers merge the two, offering a balance between quality and efficiency. Class-D amplifiers are highly efficient, using digital modulation to minimize power loss, making them ideal for compact applications. Other types may include tube amplifiers and hybrid designs, each serving specific listening preferences and applications.</p></p>
